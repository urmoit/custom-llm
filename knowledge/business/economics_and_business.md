# Economics and Business

## Microeconomics
Microeconomics studies individual economic agents: consumers, firms, and markets. Supply and demand determine prices in competitive markets. Demand curves slope downward (higher price → lower quantity demanded). Supply curves slope upward (higher price → more supplied). Equilibrium is where supply equals demand. Elasticity measures how sensitive quantity is to price changes. Consumer surplus is the benefit buyers get beyond what they pay. Producer surplus is the benefit sellers get above their minimum acceptable price.

## Macroeconomics
Macroeconomics studies the economy as a whole. GDP (Gross Domestic Product) measures total output of goods and services. GDP = C + I + G + (X−M): consumption + investment + government spending + net exports. Inflation is a general rise in prices. Deflation is falling prices. The CPI (Consumer Price Index) measures inflation. Unemployment types: frictional (between jobs), structural (skills mismatch), cyclical (economic downturns). The Federal Reserve (US) and European Central Bank set interest rates to control inflation and employment.

## Finance
Finance studies how money is managed and allocated. Time value of money: a dollar today is worth more than a dollar in the future. Present value discounts future cash flows to today's value. Interest rates, dividends, and capital gains are key return components. Risk and return are positively correlated. Diversification reduces risk without proportionally reducing return. Financial markets include stock markets, bond markets, foreign exchange, and derivatives. Key ratios: P/E (price-to-earnings), debt-to-equity, return on equity (ROE).

## Business Strategy
Business strategy defines how a company competes and creates value. Porter's five forces: competitive rivalry, threat of new entrants, threat of substitutes, bargaining power of suppliers, bargaining power of buyers. SWOT analysis: Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats. Competitive advantages can be cost leadership (lower costs), differentiation (unique value), or focus (niche market). The business model canvas describes value proposition, customer segments, channels, revenue streams, and cost structure. Disruption occurs when new entrants redefine market expectations.

## Entrepreneurship
Entrepreneurship involves identifying opportunities and building new ventures. The lean startup method emphasizes building minimal viable products (MVPs) and iterating based on user feedback. Customer discovery is the process of validating a business idea with real potential customers. Venture capital funds high-growth startups in exchange for equity. Angel investors are early-stage individuals funding startups. Key startup metrics: monthly recurring revenue (MRR), customer acquisition cost (CAC), lifetime value (LTV), churn rate. Pivoting means fundamentally changing direction based on feedback.

## Global Economy
International trade allows countries to specialize in what they produce most efficiently (comparative advantage). Free trade agreements reduce tariffs and barriers. WTO (World Trade Organization) governs international trade rules. Exchange rates determine how much one currency is worth in another. The World Bank and IMF support global financial stability and development. Supply chains became globally complex in the late 20th century; COVID-19 exposed fragility. Globalization increased economic interdependence, reduced extreme poverty in many regions, and created new competitive pressures.
