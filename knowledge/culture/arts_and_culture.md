# Culture, Arts, and Media

## Music
Music is a universal human phenomenon present in every known culture. Western music uses a 12-tone equal temperament scale. Key genres: classical (Bach, Mozart, Beethoven), jazz (blues-rooted improvisation), rock (electric guitar-driven, emerged 1950s), hip-hop (sampling, rapping, DJing — emerged Bronx, 1970s), electronic (synthesizers, drum machines). The music industry shifted from physical media to streaming (Spotify, Apple Music). Streaming pays artists per stream, raising concerns about fair compensation. Live music remains a primary revenue source for artists.

## Film and Television
Cinema began with the Lumière brothers (~1895). The Golden Age of Hollywood produced landmark films from the 1920s–1960s. Auteur theory holds that the director is the primary creative force in a film. Major film movements: Italian Neorealism, French New Wave, New Hollywood, Hong Kong action cinema. Television transformed from broadcast networks to cable to streaming. Netflix, HBO, Amazon Prime, and Disney+ transformed how content is produced and consumed. South Korean cinema (Parasite) and K-drama have achieved global popularity.

## Visual Arts
Art encompasses painting, sculpture, drawing, photography, printmaking, and digital media. Major movements: Renaissance (humanism, perspective), Baroque (drama, chiaroscuro), Impressionism (light, color, everyday subjects), Cubism (multiple perspectives simultaneously), Surrealism (the unconscious), Abstract Expressionism, Pop Art (consumer culture). Photography became art from the 19th century. Digital art, generative AI art, and NFTs have opened new creative and commercial questions. The art market is extremely lucrative with some works selling for hundreds of millions.

## Literature
Major literary traditions include Western (Greek epic, Roman poetry, Renaissance drama, novel form from 18th century), East Asian (Chinese classical literature, Japanese haiku and novel), Indian (Sanskrit epic, modern Indian literature in English), African (oral tradition, post-colonial literature). Key movements: Romanticism (emotion, nature), Realism (everyday life), Modernism (stream of consciousness, fragmentation), Postmodernism (self-referentiality, meta-fiction). Nobel Prize in Literature is awarded annually. The novel is the dominant Western literary form; short stories, poetry, and drama remain vital.

## Social Media and Digital Culture
Social media platforms have fundamentally changed communication, politics, and culture. Major platforms: Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, X (formerly Twitter), LinkedIn, Snapchat, WhatsApp. Influencer culture has made individuals with large followings commercially valuable. Viral content spreads rapidly and unpredictably. Misinformation and disinformation spread faster than corrections online. Algorithmic recommendation shapes what content billions of people see. Digital addiction and social media's effect on mental health — particularly among adolescents — are major concerns. Meme culture is a dominant form of online communication.

## Religion and Spirituality
The world's major religions: Christianity (~2.4 billion), Islam (~1.9 billion), Hinduism (~1.2 billion), Buddhism (~500 million), Judaism (~15 million). Secular/non-religious people number approximately 1 billion globally. Religion shapes ethics, art, politics, law, and social structures. The Protestant Reformation (16th century) split Christianity. The Sunni-Shia split in Islam dates to the 7th century. Hinduism is the world's oldest major religion and is highly diverse. Buddhism originated in India and spread across Asia. Judaism is a monotheistic religion and the origin of Christianity and Islam.
