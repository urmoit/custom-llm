# Chemistry

## Atomic Structure
Atoms have a nucleus of protons and neutrons surrounded by electron shells. The atomic number equals the number of protons and determines the element. The mass number is protons plus neutrons. Isotopes are atoms of the same element with different neutron counts. Electron configuration follows energy levels and orbitals: s, p, d, f.

## The Periodic Table
The periodic table organizes elements by atomic number and repeating chemical properties. Groups (columns) share similar properties. Periods (rows) represent electron shell levels. Metals occupy the left and center, nonmetals the right, and metalloids the boundary. Key groups include alkali metals (Group 1), alkaline earth metals (Group 2), halogens (Group 17), and noble gases (Group 18).

## Chemical Bonding
Ionic bonds form by electron transfer between metals and nonmetals, creating ions. Covalent bonds form by electron sharing between nonmetals. Metallic bonding involves delocalized electrons in metal lattices. Hydrogen bonds are weak intermolecular attractions important in water and biological molecules. Electronegativity differences determine bond polarity.

## Chemical Reactions
Reactions involve breaking and forming bonds. Types include: synthesis (A+B→AB), decomposition, single displacement, double displacement, combustion, and acid-base reactions. Stoichiometry uses molar ratios to calculate reactant and product quantities. The mole is 6.022×10²³ particles (Avogadro's number). Reaction rates depend on temperature, concentration, surface area, and catalysts.

## Acids and Bases
Acids donate protons (H⁺); bases accept protons. The pH scale runs from 0 (most acidic) to 14 (most basic), with 7 being neutral. Strong acids fully dissociate; weak acids partially dissociate. Buffers resist pH changes. Common acids include HCl (hydrochloric), H₂SO₄ (sulfuric), HNO₃ (nitric). Common bases include NaOH (sodium hydroxide) and NH₃ (ammonia).

## Organic Chemistry
Organic chemistry is the chemistry of carbon compounds. Hydrocarbons include alkanes (single bonds), alkenes (double bonds), alkynes (triple bonds), and aromatics (benzene rings). Functional groups (hydroxyl, carbonyl, carboxyl, amino, etc.) define compound properties. Polymers are large molecules made of repeating monomer units. Organic chemistry underlies pharmaceuticals, plastics, fuels, and biochemistry.

## Biochemistry
Biochemistry studies chemistry in living systems. The four main biomolecule classes are carbohydrates (energy and structure), lipids (membranes and energy storage), proteins (enzymes, structure, signaling), and nucleic acids (DNA, RNA, genetic information). ATP is the universal energy currency of cells. Enzymes are protein catalysts that lower activation energy without being consumed.
