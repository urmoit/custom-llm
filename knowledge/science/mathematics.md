# Mathematics

## Arithmetic and Number Theory
The natural numbers are 1, 2, 3, .... Integers include negatives and zero. Rational numbers are ratios of integers. Irrational numbers (like π and √2) cannot be expressed as fractions. Real numbers combine both. Complex numbers add imaginary unit i (where i²=−1). Prime numbers are divisible only by 1 and themselves. The fundamental theorem of arithmetic states every integer > 1 has a unique prime factorization. Modular arithmetic studies remainders.

## Algebra
Algebra generalizes arithmetic using variables. Linear equations have the form ax + b = 0. Quadratic equations have degree 2; the quadratic formula solves them. Systems of equations can be solved by substitution, elimination, or matrices. Functions map inputs to outputs. Function composition, inverses, and domains are fundamental. Polynomials are sums of terms with non-negative integer exponents. Logarithms are inverses of exponentials: log_b(x) = y means b^y = x.

## Geometry
Euclidean geometry studies flat space. Key concepts: points, lines, angles, triangles, circles, polygons, and solids. The Pythagorean theorem: in a right triangle, a² + b² = c². Pi (π ≈ 3.14159) is the ratio of circumference to diameter. Area and volume formulas apply to standard shapes. Non-Euclidean geometries (spherical, hyperbolic) apply on curved surfaces. Coordinate geometry (analytic geometry) uses algebra to describe geometry via coordinate systems.

## Calculus
Differential calculus studies rates of change. The derivative of f(x) gives the instantaneous rate of change. Integral calculus studies accumulated quantities. The definite integral gives area under a curve. The fundamental theorem of calculus connects derivatives and integrals. Limits are the foundation of calculus. Calculus is essential for physics, engineering, economics, and machine learning. Multivariable calculus extends these ideas to functions of multiple variables.

## Statistics and Probability
Statistics collects, analyzes, and interprets data. Descriptive statistics: mean, median, mode, variance, standard deviation. The normal (Gaussian) distribution is the bell curve; it appears in many natural phenomena. The central limit theorem states that sample means approach a normal distribution as sample size grows. Probability measures likelihood: from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain). Bayes' theorem updates probability given new evidence. P-values and confidence intervals are used in hypothesis testing.

## Linear Algebra
Vectors are ordered lists of numbers representing direction and magnitude. Matrices are rectangular arrays of numbers. Matrix multiplication, transpose, and inverse are fundamental operations. Determinants indicate whether a matrix is invertible. Eigenvalues and eigenvectors describe how transformations stretch or compress space. Linear algebra underlies machine learning, computer graphics, quantum mechanics, and data science. The dot product measures how parallel two vectors are.

## Discrete Mathematics
Discrete math studies countable, distinct structures. Graph theory studies vertices and edges—used in networks, routing, and social media. Combinatorics counts arrangements and selections. Boolean algebra underlies digital logic. Set theory is the foundation of mathematics. Mathematical logic studies formal proofs and inference. Trees, arrays, and graphs are discrete structures used throughout computer science.
