# Biology

## Cell Biology
The cell is the basic unit of life. Prokaryotic cells (bacteria, archaea) lack a membrane-bound nucleus. Eukaryotic cells (animals, plants, fungi, protists) have a nucleus and organelles. Key organelles include the mitochondria (energy production via ATP), ribosomes (protein synthesis), endoplasmic reticulum (protein and lipid processing), Golgi apparatus (packaging and transport), and lysosomes (waste breakdown). The cell membrane is a phospholipid bilayer controlling what enters and exits.

## Genetics and DNA
DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) stores genetic information in sequences of four bases: adenine (A), thymine (T), guanine (G), and cytosine (C). A pairs with T; G pairs with C. DNA is organized into chromosomes. Genes are segments of DNA encoding proteins. The central dogma: DNA is transcribed to RNA, which is translated to proteins. Mutations are changes in DNA sequence. Humans have 46 chromosomes (23 pairs) and approximately 20,000–25,000 genes.

## Evolution
Evolution is the change in heritable traits in populations over generations. Charles Darwin proposed natural selection: individuals with advantageous traits are more likely to survive and reproduce. Evidence for evolution comes from fossils, comparative anatomy, molecular biology, and direct observation. Speciation occurs when populations become reproductively isolated. Life on Earth shares a common ancestor estimated to have existed about 3.5–4 billion years ago.

## Ecology
Ecology studies relationships between organisms and their environments. A population is a group of the same species in an area. A community is all populations in an area. An ecosystem includes the community plus the physical environment. Trophic levels describe feeding relationships: producers (plants), primary consumers (herbivores), secondary and tertiary consumers (carnivores), and decomposers. Nutrient cycles (carbon, nitrogen, water) are essential to ecosystem function.

## Human Biology
The human body has 11 major organ systems: skeletal, muscular, nervous, endocrine, cardiovascular, lymphatic, respiratory, digestive, urinary, reproductive, and integumentary. The brain has about 86 billion neurons. The heart beats about 100,000 times per day. The liver performs over 500 metabolic functions. The immune system distinguishes self from non-self and defends against pathogens. Homeostasis maintains stable internal conditions despite external changes.

## Microbiology
Bacteria are single-celled prokaryotes found in virtually every environment. Viruses are not technically alive—they require host cells to replicate. Viruses consist of genetic material in a protein coat. Antibiotics treat bacterial infections but have no effect on viruses. Antibiotic resistance is a major global health challenge. Fungi can be unicellular (yeast) or multicellular (molds, mushrooms). Many microorganisms are beneficial—the human gut microbiome contains trillions of bacteria essential to health.
