# India

## Identity and Core Facts
India is the world's most populous country with approximately 1.44 billion people. It is the seventh largest country by area (3.29 million km²). Capital: New Delhi. Largest city: Mumbai. Currency: Indian Rupee (INR). India is a federal parliamentary democratic republic — the world's largest democracy. Official languages at the national level include Hindi and English; 22 scheduled languages are recognized.

## Government
India has a parliamentary system with a bicameral Parliament: the Lok Sabha (lower house, directly elected) and the Rajya Sabha (upper house). The President is head of state; the Prime Minister is head of government. India has 28 states and 8 union territories. The Supreme Court is the apex judicial body. India's constitution is the longest written constitution of any sovereign nation.

## Economy
India has the world's fifth largest economy by nominal GDP and third largest by PPP. Major sectors: IT and software services (Bangalore is a global tech hub), pharmaceuticals, agriculture, manufacturing, and textiles. India is one of the world's largest exporters of software services. The IT sector (Infosys, TCS, Wipro) is globally significant. India has a large and growing startup ecosystem. Rapid economic growth is transforming the country; India is expected to become the third largest economy by the 2030s.

## History
One of the world's oldest civilizations — the Indus Valley Civilization dates to ~2500 BCE. Major empires: Maurya (Ashoka), Gupta, Mughal. British colonization began in the 18th century. India gained independence on August 15, 1947. Partition created Pakistan alongside independent India. Mahatma Gandhi's nonviolent independence movement is a landmark in world history.

## Culture and Society
India is extraordinarily diverse: hundreds of languages, multiple major religions, varied cuisines, and regional cultures. Hinduism (~80%), Islam (~14%), Christianity, Sikhism, Buddhism, and Jainism are major religions. Bollywood is one of the world's largest film industries. Indian cuisine, yoga, Ayurveda, and classical arts have global reach. Cricket is the dominant sport. India has 42 UNESCO World Heritage Sites.
