# France

## Identity and Core Facts
France (French Republic) is a semi-presidential republic in Western Europe. Population: approximately 68 million. Capital: Paris. Currency: Euro (€). France is one of the founding members of the EU and a permanent member of the UN Security Council. It is the world's most visited country by international tourists (~90 million/year pre-COVID).

## Government
France has a semi-presidential system: both a President (strong executive, elected by popular vote, 5-year term) and a Prime Minister (head of government). The Parliament has two chambers: the National Assembly (lower) and the Senate (upper). France has 18 administrative regions. The Republic's motto is Liberté, Égalité, Fraternité. France is a member of NATO, the EU, the G7, and the G20.

## Economy and Industry
France has one of Europe's largest economies. Key sectors: aerospace (Airbus), luxury goods (LVMH, Hermès, L'Oréal), automotive (Renault, Peugeot), energy (EDF, nuclear power), agriculture, tourism, and finance. France generates about 70% of its electricity from nuclear power — the highest share globally. Paris is a major global financial and business center. The French agricultural sector (wine, cheese, wheat) is the EU's largest.

## History
France was unified as a kingdom by the late medieval period. The French Revolution (1789) transformed Europe with ideas of liberty, equality, and secularism. Napoleon Bonaparte spread revolutionary law across Europe. France was a major colonial power in Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Caribbean. It suffered enormous losses in both World Wars. Charles de Gaulle led the Free French during WWII and founded the Fifth Republic in 1958.

## Culture
French culture has had enormous global influence. French cuisine is UNESCO-listed as intangible cultural heritage; wine and cheese are iconic. The Louvre is the world's most visited museum. French literature: Voltaire, Molière, Victor Hugo, Camus, Sartre. French cinema is celebrated globally (New Wave directors Godard, Truffaut). Fashion: Paris is the world fashion capital. Philosophy: Descartes, Pascal, Rousseau, Simone de Beauvoir.
