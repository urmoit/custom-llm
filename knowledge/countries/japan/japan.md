# Japan

## Identity and Core Facts
Japan is an island nation in East Asia consisting of four main islands (Honshu, Hokkaido, Kyushu, Shikoku) and about 6,800 smaller islands. Population: approximately 125 million. Capital: Tokyo (world's most populous metropolitan area, ~37 million). Currency: Japanese Yen (JPY). Japan is a constitutional monarchy with a parliamentary system; the Emperor is head of state, the Prime Minister heads the government.

## Economy
Japan has the world's fourth largest economy by nominal GDP. Key industries: automobiles (Toyota, Honda, Nissan), electronics (Sony, Panasonic, Toshiba), robotics, precision manufacturing, and chemicals. Japan is a world leader in robotics and automation. "Made in Japan" became synonymous with high quality in the postwar era. Japan pioneered lean manufacturing (Toyota Production System, Kaizen). The economy faced prolonged stagnation after a 1990 asset bubble collapse — the "Lost Decade" (actually two+ decades).

## History
Japan's recorded history extends over 2,000 years. The feudal era (samurai, shogunate) lasted until the Meiji Restoration (1868), which rapidly modernized Japan. Japan became an imperial power, colonizing Korea and Taiwan. World War II ended with Japan's surrender after atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki (August 1945). The postwar US occupation facilitated Japan's democratic constitution (1947). Japan experienced extraordinary economic growth 1955–1990 ("Japanese economic miracle").

## Culture
Japan has one of the world's most distinctive cultures. Key concepts: wabi-sabi (beauty in imperfection), ikigai (reason for being), omotenashi (selfless hospitality). Traditional arts: tea ceremony, ikebana (flower arrangement), calligraphy, noh and kabuki theater, haiku poetry. Pop culture: anime, manga, video games (Nintendo, Sony PlayStation) have massive global influence. Japanese cuisine (sushi, ramen, tempura, wagyu) is globally celebrated. Japan has the world's highest life expectancy (~84 years).

## Society
Japan is a highly homogeneous society (~98% ethnic Japanese) with very low immigration historically. Declining birth rate and aging population are major long-term challenges. Work culture is known for dedication ("salaryman" culture) but also karoshi (death from overwork) concerns. Japan is one of the safest countries in the world with extremely low crime rates. Public transportation, especially the Shinkansen (bullet train) network, is among the world's best.
