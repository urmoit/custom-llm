# Brazil

## Identity and Core Facts
Brazil (Federative Republic of Brazil) is the largest country in South America and the fifth largest in the world by both area (8.5 million km²) and population (~215 million). Capital: Brasília. Largest city: São Paulo. Currency: Brazilian Real (BRL). Official language: Portuguese. Brazil is a federal presidential constitutional republic.

## Economy
Brazil has Latin America's largest economy and one of the world's largest by GDP. Key sectors: agriculture (world's largest exporter of coffee, orange juice, soybeans, and beef), mining (iron ore, oil via Petrobras), manufacturing, and a growing technology sector. São Paulo is Latin America's financial capital. Brazil discovered massive offshore oil reserves (pre-salt) in the 2000s. Income inequality (Gini coefficient) remains very high despite progress.

## History and Politics
Brazil was colonized by Portugal from 1500. It gained independence peacefully in 1822 as an empire. The republic was proclaimed in 1889. A military dictatorship ruled 1964–1985. Democracy was restored with the 1988 constitution. Brazil hosted the 2014 FIFA World Cup and 2016 Summer Olympics. Political instability, corruption scandals (Lava Jato investigation), and economic cycles have been recurring challenges.

## Geography and Environment
Brazil contains approximately 60% of the Amazon rainforest — the world's largest tropical rainforest and a critical carbon sink and biodiversity hotspot. Deforestation is a major environmental and geopolitical issue. Brazil has an extraordinary diversity of ecosystems: Amazon, Cerrado savanna, Pantanal wetland, Atlantic Forest, and Caatinga semi-arid. It is one of the world's most megadiverse countries.

## Culture
Brazilian culture is a blend of Indigenous, African, and Portuguese influences. Carnival (Carnaval) in Rio de Janeiro is one of the world's biggest festivals. Samba and bossa nova are globally influential music genres. Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu and Capoeira are martial arts of Brazilian origin. Football (soccer) is a national passion — Brazil has won the FIFA World Cup five times, the most of any nation. Cuisine includes feijoada, açaí, churrasco, and pão de queijo.
