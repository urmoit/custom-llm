# China

## Identity and Core Facts
The People's Republic of China is the world's most populous country (approximately 1.4 billion) and third largest by area (9.6 million km²). Capital: Beijing. Largest city and economic center: Shanghai. Currency: Renminbi (yuan, CNY). Official language: Standard Mandarin (Putonghua). China is a single-party state governed by the Chinese Communist Party (CCP).

## Government
China is governed by the CCP under a system of socialist market economy and single-party rule. The National People's Congress is the formal legislative body. The President (also General Secretary of the CCP) holds supreme authority. China operates a "one country, two systems" arrangement with Hong Kong (until 2047) and Macau. Taiwan is administered separately but claimed by the PRC. China is a permanent member of the UN Security Council.

## Economy
China has the world's second-largest economy by nominal GDP and largest by purchasing power parity (PPP). China became the "world's factory" from the 1990s, dominating global manufacturing. Key sectors: manufacturing, exports, technology, construction, and increasingly domestic consumption and services. Major companies: Alibaba, Tencent, Huawei, BYD, Xiaomi. China is the world's largest exporter and second-largest importer. The Belt and Road Initiative is a massive global infrastructure investment program.

## History
Chinese civilization is one of the world's oldest, dating back over 3,500 years. Major dynasties: Shang, Zhou, Qin (unified China, Great Wall begun), Han, Tang, Song, Ming, Qing. The Republic of China was established in 1912. The PRC was founded by Mao Zedong in 1949 following the Communist Revolution. The Cultural Revolution (1966–76) caused massive social upheaval. Economic reforms under Deng Xiaoping from 1978 launched China's modern economic growth.

## Culture and Society
China has extraordinary cultural diversity with 56 recognized ethnic groups; Han Chinese are about 92% of the population. Confucianism, Taoism, and Buddhism deeply shape culture and values. Chinese cuisine varies dramatically by region. The Chinese New Year (Spring Festival) is the world's largest annual human migration event. China has the world's largest internet user base. Mandarin Chinese is the most spoken language in the world by native speakers.
