# United Kingdom

## Identity and Core Facts
The United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland is a constitutional monarchy and parliamentary democracy in northwestern Europe. It consists of four nations: England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland. Capital: London. Currency: Pound sterling (GBP). Population: approximately 67 million. The monarch is head of state; the Prime Minister leads the government.

## Government
The UK Parliament has two houses: the House of Commons (elected) and the House of Lords (appointed). The Westminster system of parliamentary democracy has been adopted by many countries. Devolved parliaments exist in Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland with varying powers. The UK is a founding member of NATO and a permanent member of the UN Security Council. Brexit (2020) saw the UK leave the European Union.

## History
England unified by the 11th century. The UK became a global empire through the 17th–19th centuries, once controlling about a quarter of the world's land. The Industrial Revolution began in Britain in the late 18th century. Britain played a central role in both World Wars. Post-WW2 decolonization dramatically reduced the empire. The UK joined the European Economic Community in 1973 and left the EU in 2020.

## Economy
The UK has one of the world's largest economies. Key sectors: financial services (London is a global financial center), pharmaceuticals, aerospace, creative industries, education, and professional services. The City of London is a leading global hub for banking and finance. Edinburgh is a major financial center in Scotland. The NHS (National Health Service) provides universal healthcare.

## Culture
British culture has had enormous global influence through the English language, literature (Shakespeare, Dickens, Rowling), music (the Beatles, Rolling Stones, Adele), film, and television. Oxford and Cambridge are among the world's most prestigious universities. Football (soccer) was codified in England and is the world's most popular sport. Tea culture, pubs, and dry humor are iconic British cultural elements.
