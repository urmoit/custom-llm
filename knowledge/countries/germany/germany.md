# Germany

## Identity and Core Facts
Germany (Federal Republic of Germany) is a federal parliamentary republic in central-western Europe. Population: approximately 84 million — the most populous EU member state. Capital: Berlin. Currency: Euro (€). Germany consists of 16 federal states (Länder). Germany is a founding member of the EU and NATO.

## Government and Politics
Germany has a parliamentary system. The Bundestag (federal parliament) is the main legislative body. The Chancellor is head of government; the President is a largely ceremonial head of state. The Federal Constitutional Court protects the Basic Law (constitution). Germany's postwar constitution was designed with specific safeguards against authoritarianism. The political landscape features the CDU/CSU, SPD, Greens, FDP, and AfD as major parties.

## Economy
Germany has Europe's largest and the world's third or fourth largest economy. Key sectors: automobiles (Volkswagen, BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Porsche), machinery, chemicals (BASF), electronics (Siemens, SAP), and financial services. Germany is the world's third largest exporter. The "Mittelstand" — small and medium-sized businesses — forms the backbone of the economy. Germany has a strong tradition of vocational training (dual education system). The Ruhr Valley was historically Europe's industrial heartland.

## History
German unification under Prussia occurred in 1871. Germany's defeat in WWI and the harsh Treaty of Versailles contributed to the rise of Nazism. Nazi Germany (1933–1945) under Hitler perpetrated the Holocaust and ignited WWII, causing tens of millions of deaths. After WWII, Germany was divided into West Germany (democratic, NATO-aligned) and East Germany (communist, Warsaw Pact). German reunification occurred on October 3, 1990, following the fall of the Berlin Wall (1989).

## Culture
Germany has made enormous contributions to Western civilization: philosophy (Kant, Hegel, Nietzsche, Marx), music (Bach, Beethoven, Brahms, Wagner), literature (Goethe, Schiller, Kafka), science (Einstein, Planck, Heisenberg), and printing (Gutenberg). Beer culture (Oktoberfest, over 1,300 breweries) is internationally famous. Football (soccer) is the dominant sport. Germany has world-class museums, opera houses, and cultural institutions.
