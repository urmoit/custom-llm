# Sports

## Football (Soccer)
Association football (soccer) is the world's most popular sport with an estimated 4 billion fans. FIFA governs the sport globally. The FIFA World Cup (every 4 years) is the world's most watched sporting event. Key leagues: English Premier League, La Liga (Spain), Bundesliga (Germany), Serie A (Italy), Ligue 1 (France). UEFA Champions League is the top European club competition. The GOAT debate centers on Lionel Messi (Argentina, 8 Ballon d'Or, 2022 World Cup winner) and Cristiano Ronaldo (Portugal, 5 Ballon d'Or). Brazil has won the most World Cups (5). Positions: goalkeeper, defenders, midfielders, forwards.

## American Football (NFL)
The NFL (National Football League) has 32 teams in two conferences (AFC, NFC). The Super Bowl is the NFL championship game and the most-watched annual US sporting event. Teams play a 17-game regular season plus playoffs. Key positions: quarterback (most important), running back, wide receiver, offensive/defensive linemen, linebacker, cornerback, safety. Tom Brady won 7 Super Bowls. The Draft selects college players annually. Concussion and CTE (chronic traumatic encephalopathy) are major player safety concerns.

## Basketball (NBA)
The NBA has 30 teams. The season runs October–June, with 82 regular-season games per team. Playoffs involve 16 teams in a bracket format. Key skills: shooting, dribbling, passing, defense, rebounding. The three-point line revolutionized modern play. Michael Jordan (6 championships, 6 Finals MVPs) and LeBron James (4 championships) headline the GOAT debate. International players now account for about 25% of NBA rosters. The NBA G League is the primary development league. WNBA is the women's counterpart.

## Tennis
The four Grand Slams are the Australian Open, French Open (Roland Garros), Wimbledon, and US Open. Novak Djokovic holds the men's record with 24 Grand Slam titles. Serena Williams won 23 Grand Slams — most in the Open Era for women. Rafael Nadal dominated clay (14 French Open titles). Roger Federer won 20 Grand Slams and is celebrated for his elegant style. Surfaces: grass (Wimbledon), clay (Roland Garros), hard court (Australian and US Open). ATP (men) and WTA (women) govern professional tennis.

## Olympics
The Olympic Games are held every four years. The Summer Olympics are typically larger; the Winter Olympics focus on snow and ice sports. The IOC (International Olympic Committee) governs the Games. The Olympic motto: "Faster, Higher, Stronger — Together." The most decorated Olympian is Michael Phelps (28 medals, 23 gold). The Games have been political — boycotts (1980, 1984), doping controversies, and debates over host city human rights records have all featured. The Paris 2024 Summer Olympics was a major global event.

## Cricket
Cricket is especially popular in South Asia, Australia, England, the Caribbean, and Southern Africa. Formats: Test cricket (5 days, traditional), ODI (One Day International, 50 overs), T20 (20 overs, fastest format). The ICC (International Cricket Council) governs the sport. India's cricket board (BCCI) is the world's richest sports body. The IPL (Indian Premier League) T20 competition attracts the highest-paid cricket players. Key batting/bowling records: Sachin Tendulkar holds most ODI and Test runs. The Ashes is the historic England-Australia rivalry.

## eSports and Gaming
Competitive gaming (eSports) has grown into a multi-billion-dollar industry. Major titles: League of Legends, Dota 2, Counter-Strike 2, Valorant, Fortnite, FIFA/EA FC, and fighting games. The League of Legends World Championship draws tens of millions of viewers. Prize pools for The International (Dota 2) have exceeded $40 million. South Korea pioneered professional gaming culture. eSports athletes face similar pressures to traditional athletes: team dynamics, coaching, training regimens, burnout, and short careers.
