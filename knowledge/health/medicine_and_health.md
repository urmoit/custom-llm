# Health and Medicine

## Nutrition
Macronutrients: carbohydrates (4 kcal/g, primary energy), proteins (4 kcal/g, building and repair), fats (9 kcal/g, energy storage and hormones). Micronutrients include vitamins and minerals essential in small amounts. Vitamin C prevents scurvy; Vitamin D is essential for bone health; B12 is critical for nerve function. Iron carries oxygen in blood. Calcium and phosphorus form bones. The Mediterranean diet and DASH diet have strong evidence for long-term health. Ultra-processed foods are linked to increased risk of obesity, diabetes, and heart disease.

## Exercise and Fitness
Regular physical activity reduces risk of heart disease, type 2 diabetes, obesity, depression, and some cancers. WHO recommends 150–300 minutes of moderate aerobic activity per week for adults. Strength training two or more days per week is recommended. Exercise types: aerobic (running, cycling), anaerobic (weightlifting), flexibility (yoga, stretching), balance. VO2 max measures aerobic capacity. HIIT (high-intensity interval training) improves fitness efficiently. Rest and recovery are as important as training itself.

## Common Diseases
Heart disease is the leading cause of death globally. Hypertension (high blood pressure) is a major risk factor for heart disease and stroke. Type 2 diabetes is driven by insulin resistance and is largely preventable through lifestyle. Cancer involves uncontrolled cell growth; major types include lung, breast, colon, and prostate. Alzheimer's disease is the most common form of dementia. Depression and anxiety are the most prevalent mental health disorders. Obesity is a complex disease with metabolic, psychological, and environmental factors.

## Pharmacology
Drugs alter biological processes. Pharmacokinetics studies how the body absorbs, distributes, metabolizes, and excretes drugs. Pharmacodynamics studies drug effects. Antibiotics kill or inhibit bacteria; overuse causes resistance. Analgesics (painkillers) include NSAIDs (ibuprofen), acetaminophen, and opioids. Statins lower cholesterol. Antidepressants include SSRIs (selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors). Vaccines prime the immune system to recognize pathogens without causing disease. Generic drugs contain the same active ingredient as brand-name but are cheaper.

## Mental Health
Mental health encompasses emotional, psychological, and social wellbeing. Depression is characterized by persistent low mood, loss of interest, and fatigue. Anxiety disorders involve excessive fear or worry. Bipolar disorder involves episodes of depression and mania. Schizophrenia involves distorted reality, hallucinations, and disordered thinking. PTSD follows traumatic events. Treatment options include psychotherapy (CBT, DBT), medication, and lifestyle changes. The stigma around mental health has decreased significantly in recent decades. Sleep deprivation severely impairs cognitive function and mental health.

## Medical Procedures and Diagnostics
Blood tests measure complete blood count (CBC), metabolic panels, lipids, hormones, and markers like HbA1c. Imaging includes X-ray, CT scan, MRI, PET scan, and ultrasound—each suited to different purposes. Electrocardiogram (ECG) records heart electrical activity. Endoscopy views internal organs using a flexible camera. Surgery types: open, laparoscopic (minimally invasive), robotic-assisted. Anesthesia blocks pain during procedures: local, regional, or general. Evidence-based medicine relies on clinical trials and systematic reviews to guide treatment decisions.

## Public Health
Public health focuses on improving health at the population level. Vaccination programs have eradicated smallpox and nearly eliminated polio. Sanitation and clean water access dramatically reduced infectious disease mortality in the 20th century. Epidemiology studies how diseases spread. The basic reproduction number (R₀) indicates how many people one case infects. Herd immunity occurs when enough of a population is immune to slow disease spread. Global health organizations include the WHO, CDC, and MSF (Doctors Without Borders).
